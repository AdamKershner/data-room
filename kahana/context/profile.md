## The Company

**Company:** Kahana
**Product:** Oasis Browser
**Website:** kahana.co
**Stage:** Early GTM / pre-revenue (first enterprise pilots in progress)
**HQ:** [City, Country]

**What we do:**
Oasis is an AI-native desktop browser built on Firefox. Knowledge workers use natural language — text and voice — to control their entire browser environment. Commands like "open tabs 3 and 4 in split view," "create a tab group for this project," or "close all background tabs" execute instantly. The result: zero time spent managing browser state, more time in flow.

**What makes us different:**
The only browser where the interface is natural language. Every other productivity browser makes tab management easier through UI; we make it disappear entirely. Built on Firefox for privacy-first foundations — no Google surveillance layer.

## Market Context

**Category:** AI-native enterprise browser / productivity browser
**Market size:** Enterprise browser market estimated $X (see competitor radar for landscape context)

**Key trends driving demand:**
- Knowledge worker productivity is the new enterprise IT priority — tab overload is measurable, studied, and boardroom-level now
- AI-native products expected as default in 2025+ tools — "AI-powered" is table stakes; natural language interface is the differentiator
- Enterprise security teams adopting managed browser deployments (Island, Chrome Enterprise trend) — Oasis enters this conversation with a productivity-first angle

**Primary competitors:**

| Competitor | Their strength | Our edge |
|------------|---------------|----------|
| Island Browser | Enterprise security, governance, compliance | We win on productivity, AI UX, PLG adoption — they require IT-led deployment |
| Arc Browser | Beautiful consumer UX, strong word-of-mouth | We win on enterprise features, voice control, B2B deployment — Arc has no enterprise story |
| Chrome Enterprise | Google ecosystem integration, ubiquity | We win on privacy (Firefox), AI natural language, flow state — lose when Google lock-in is non-negotiable |
| Here Browser | Similar "reduce toggle tax" positioning | We win on AI-nativeness, PLG ease, voice control — they're more complex workflow automation |
