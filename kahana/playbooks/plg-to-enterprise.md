# Playbook: PLG → Enterprise Conversion

*The B2C2B flywheel in action. How to turn individual Oasis users into enterprise deals. This playbook is unique to Oasis's motion and is your primary pipeline source at this stage.*

---

## What This Playbook Is For

Oasis's go-to-market strategy is B2C2B: individuals adopt at $20/month, and that adoption becomes the signal and the proof of value that justifies a $50K+ enterprise pilot. This is not a cold sales motion — you're converting people who already believe in the product.

The job here is not to convince someone Oasis is good. It's to help them formalize what's already happening.

---

## Trigger Conditions

Run this playbook when ANY of the following are true:

| Trigger | Threshold | Priority |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| Paywall hit by user at ICP-qualified company | Any | Immediate |
| 3+ users from same company domain active | 3 users | Immediate |
| 2 users from same domain + pricing page visit | Both | High |
| Individual user active 3+ days/week for 4+ weeks | Sustained usage | High |
| Individual user invited a teammate | Any invite | High |

## Step 1: Assess the Situation (5 min)

Before reaching out, understand the state of adoption at this account.

Check HubSpot for the company domain:
- How many active users from this domain?
- Which features have they used? (Core activation, split view, tab groups, voice commands)
- How long have they been on Oasis?
- Has anyone hit the paywall?
- Did anyone come from a demo request or pricing page?
- Who is the most senior person from this domain?

This takes 5 minutes and determines everything about how you frame the outreach.

---

## Step 2: Identify the Right Person to Talk To

The conversation depends on where adoption is:

**Scenario A: One power user**
Talk to that user directly. They're self-selected as the champion. Your job: help them understand the team tier and give them language to advocate internally.

**Scenario B: 2–4 users, no clear champion**
Identify the most senior title. Reach out to them — they may not know their colleagues are using Oasis. Your job: surface what's already happening and frame the enterprise pilot.

**Scenario C: 3+ users, organic spread**
This is a warm deal. Reach out to the highest seniority ESA or engineering lead. Reference that you see multiple colleagues are already using it. Don't explain what Oasis is — they know. Jump straight to the team conversation.

---

## Step 3: Frame the Outreach

The PLG-to-enterprise outreach should feel like a conversation continuation, not a new pitch.

**What to include:**
- Reference what's already happening (specific users, features used)
- Acknowledge they've already validated the value — don't resell the product
- Name the transition: "moving from individual accounts to a team setup"
- Make the next step frictionless: a 20-minute call to scope what a pilot looks like

**What NOT to include:**
- Product pitch or feature descriptions — they already know
- Generic ROI claims — they're sold; you need to help them build the business case
- A long email — they're users, not cold prospects

---

## Step 4: The Enterprise Pilot Conversation

When you get the call, this is the agenda:

**Understand the current state:**
- How many people from the team are using Oasis?
- What's working well? What's missing?
- Who else on the team has the same browser management pain?

**Scope the pilot:**
- A $50K pilot is not just "more seats" — it includes onboarding support, admin controls (when available), and dedicated support during the pilot period
- Define success criteria upfront: what does the team need to see in 30/60 days to justify the full deployment?
- Identify the economic buyer (VP Eng, CTO, or whoever approves $50K+) and map a path to them

**Handle the IT question:**
- Proactively raise the IT/security question before they do — "I want to flag the IT deployment piece now so there are no surprises: Oasis is built on Firefox, which your IT team already knows how to manage via MDM or Group Policy. I can get them documentation and we can do a joint call."

---

## Step 5: Build the Business Case

After the discovery call, send a written summary of the pilot scope. This gives your champion something to show the economic buyer.

```
Read context/personas/vp-engineering.md to understand what the economic buyer cares about.
Build a 1-page pilot proposal for [company] based on:
- [N] users currently active
- [Features used]
- Proposed pilot: [N] seats for [timeframe] at $[amount]
- Success criteria agreed on call
- IT deployment approach
Format: short enough to forward in an email, specific enough to drive a decision.
```

---

## Step 6: Manage the Deal

This is a real sales deal — not a self-serve upgrade. Treat it accordingly.

- Create an opportunity in HubSpot with the deal value
- Map all stakeholders: champion (ESA), economic buyer (VP Eng), IT gatekeeper
- Set up a mutual action plan with the champion: what do they need, in what order, to get to a signed pilot?
- Check in weekly with the champion — they're the one carrying this internally
- Don't disappear between touches; these deals move fast when the champion is engaged

## Notes

- **Speed matters.** The window when a user has just hit the paywall is the best time to call. They're already thinking about upgrading — you're not interrupting, you're helping.
- **Don't over-engineer the proposal.** At this stage, a one-page email scope is better than a 20-slide deck. You're closing a pilot, not a $500K enterprise deal.
- **Log everything in HubSpot.** Every PLG-to-enterprise conversion is data — what signal fired, how long it took, what the objections were. This data shapes the signal library and campaign strategy.
