# CLAUDE.md — Oasis Browser (by Kahana)

Oasis Browser eliminates context switching and cognitive load for knowledge workers through AI-powered natural language and voice control of their browser environment. Built on Firefox. Individual subscriptions ($20/mo) seed enterprise adoption — B2C2B motion.

---

## Company

**Company:** Kahana | **Product:** Oasis Browser
**Website:** kahana.co
**Stage:** Pre-revenue / early GTM — 4-person GTM team
**HQ:** [City]

**What we do:** Oasis is an AI-native desktop browser built on Firefox. Knowledge workers control their entire browser environment with natural language — text or voice commands like "open tabs 3 and 4 in split view," "create tab group for this project," or "close all background tabs." We eliminate the manual overhead of window management so people can stay in flow.

**GTM motion:** B2C2B — individual $20/mo subscriptions drive organic team adoption; enterprise pilots start at $50,000/year
**ACV:** $20/mo (consumer) → $50,000+/year (enterprise pilot)
**Primary channels:** PLG self-serve → product-qualified outbound → inbound intent (Warmly)

---

## ICP

Full definition: `context/icp-definition.md`

**Who we sell to:** B2B SaaS companies (50–500 employees) where knowledge workers — especially Enterprise Solutions Architects, engineers, and technical operators — spend 6+ hours/day in a browser across multiple projects, tools, and environments.

**Tier 1 (Top 150 accounts):** 100–500 employee SaaS companies with dedicated ESA, Solutions Engineering, or Platform Engineering teams; Series A–C; ≥3 Oasis users already active from the same domain

**Tier 2 (300–600 accounts):** 50–250 employee SaaS startups; engineering or solutions teams; ≥1 Oasis user active or pricing page visited

**Tier 3 (automated):** 20–150 employee SaaS companies; any individual trial activity from their domain

**Never target:**
- Non-tech companies or hardware-first orgs — browser productivity pain doesn't translate
- Google Workspace-mandated shops where Chrome is policy-enforced — blocker too high
- Pure consumer or media companies — knowledge worker density too low
- Procurement/compliance-gated enterprises (5,000+ employees) — motion too slow for current stage

## Positioning

Full document: `context/positioning.md`

**One line:** The only browser that understands natural language — so knowledge workers spend zero time managing tabs and 100% of time on actual work.

**We win when:** The primary pain is cognitive load and context switching — not security/compliance
**We lose when:** CISO or security team drives the evaluation (Island, LayerX win on compliance features we don't prioritize yet)

vs. Island/Chrome Enterprise: We win on AI productivity and natural language UX; they win on governance
vs. Arc/SigmaOS: We win on enterprise management layer, voice control, B2B deployment; they're consumer-only
vs. Here Browser: We win on AI-nativeness and PLG adoption ease; they win on complex workflow automation
vs. Chrome (default): We win on privacy (Firefox base), AI commands, flow state — lose when Google lock-in is non-negotiable

**Voice:** Direct, peer-to-peer. We talk to engineers and architects, not executives. No marketing fluff. Lead with the specific productivity cost of context switching, not vague "AI-powered" language.

## Signals

Full library: `context/signal-library.md`

**Act immediately (Tier 1 — within 48 hours):**
1. **Paywall hit** — PLG user hit usage limits; high purchase intent, reach out within 24h
2. **3+ users from same company domain active in Oasis** — organic team adoption signal; trigger enterprise outreach
3. **Demo request or Contact Sales form submitted** — highest intent, 15-min SLA

**Add to sequence (Tier 2):**
1. **Pricing page visit** — individual or company rep evaluating upgrade path
2. **ESA / Solutions Engineer hire at target account** — new person inheriting context-switching pain, change appetite high
3. **Series A or B announced at target SaaS company** — headcount scaling, browser chaos about to hit, budget available
4. **Warmly website visitor identification** — company visited kahana.co, didn't convert; trigger Tier 2 outbound
