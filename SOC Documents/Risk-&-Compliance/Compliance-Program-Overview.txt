1. Purpose
This document provides an overview of Kahana’s information security and compliance program for the Kahana Oasis platform. It explains how policies, processes, and controls are organized and governed to meet SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria (Security, and any additional criteria in scope).

This document is Kahana-specific and references the data-room SOC 2 gap analysis artifacts used to prioritize remediation and audit readiness work.

2. Governance and Roles
Executive Oversight: CEO Adam Kershner and CTO are ultimately accountable for security and compliance outcomes and approve key policies, risk decisions, and SOC 2 scope.

Security / Compliance Lead: Owns the information security program, coordinates SOC 2 readiness, manages risk assessments, and oversees incident response, vendor risk, and control operation.

Engineering & DevOps: Implement and operate technical controls (access, logging, SDLC, change management, backups/DR, vulnerability management).

HR / People: Manage hiring, background checks, onboarding/offboarding, security awareness training, and the Employee Handbook.

Operations / Legal: Oversee physical security, vendor and contract terms, and legal/privacy obligations.

4. Risk Management Process
Kahana’s risk management approach includes:

Annual Risk Assessment Report: Formal identification and evaluation of risks to Kahana Oasis and supporting systems, with likelihood/impact ratings and prioritized risks.

Risk Register: Living register tracking each risk’s description, rating, owner, actions, and status.

Risk Treatment Plan: Mapping of risks to treatment decisions (mitigate, accept, transfer, avoid) with specific mitigation tasks, targets, and residual risk ratings.

Results are reviewed with leadership at least annually and after major changes or incidents.

4.1 Gap Reference Baseline (Data-Room)
Kahana tracks prioritized remediation using the SOC 2 gap baseline (P0/P1/P2), including:
- P0: quarterly access reviews not yet operating with sign-off logs
- P0: Supabase Free plan backup gap (upgrade to Pro or equivalent documented backup process)
- P1: no org-wide internal IdP (documented deferral)
- P1: no dedicated SIEM (current state documented; Datadog under consideration)
- P2: risk assessment execution and documentation completeness

These references are maintained in the SOC2 Documentation Review Summary and Controls Matrix documents in this repository.

5. Control Framework and Mapping (CC1–CC9)
Kahana operates a Controls Matrix that:

Lists all key controls (policies, processes, technical safeguards).

Maps them to SOC 2 Common Criteria (CC1–CC9) and any additional criteria in scope.

Identifies control owners, frequency, and typical evidence.

This matrix is used to plan evidence collection and to verify that all relevant SOC 2 criteria have at least one control in place.

6. Evidence Management and Audit Readiness
To support SOC 2 Type I & II:

Evidence (screenshots, exports, reports, tickets, logs, training records, completed checklists) is collected and stored in organized, access‑controlled locations.

Each control in the matrix has defined “evidence examples” so teams know what to capture during the audit period.

Internal spot checks or informal “mock audits” may be performed periodically to validate that controls operate as described and evidence is available.

7. Training, Communication, and Culture
Security expectations and key policies are communicated via onboarding, the Employee Handbook, and the Security Awareness Training Program.

Annual training and phishing simulations reinforce secure behavior.

Employees sign acknowledgments for the Handbook, Code of Conduct, and confidentiality obligations.

Management and Security provide regular updates on security posture, incidents, and policy changes.

8. Continuous Improvement
Kahana’s compliance program is designed to improve over time through:

Lessons learned from incidents and near‑misses.

Feedback from engineers, staff, and customers.

Findings from risk assessments, vendor reviews, and SOC 2 audits.

Updates to policies, controls, and processes are tracked, approved, and communicated, and the Compliance Program Documentation is reviewed at least annually.
