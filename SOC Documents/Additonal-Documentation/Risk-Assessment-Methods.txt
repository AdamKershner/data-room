Document Title: Risk Assessment Policy – Kahana Oasis

Owner: Security Lead / Compliance Lead
Approved By: CTO / CEO
Effective Date: March 2026

1. Purpose
This Risk Assessment Policy defines how Kahana identifies, evaluates, and treats information security and operational risks affecting Kahana Oasis and supporting systems. The objective is to apply a consistent, repeatable process that supports business decisions, customer trust, and SOC 2 readiness.

2. Scope
This policy applies to:
- Kahana Oasis production and supporting environments (AWS, Supabase, CI/CD, auth, logging, backups/DR, and critical SaaS tools)
- People, processes, and vendors involved in delivering and operating Oasis
- Security, availability, confidentiality, and compliance risks that could affect customer or company data

3. SOC 2 Alignment
This policy supports SOC 2 risk management expectations (including CC9.x concepts) by requiring:
- A formal recurring risk assessment process
- Documented risk ratings and treatment decisions
- Leadership review and approval
- Evidence of progress through risk register/treatment tracking

4. Risk Assessment Cadence
Kahana conducts risk assessment on the following schedule:
- Annual baseline assessment: at least once per year
- Trigger-based reassessment: after material changes or incidents, including:
  - major architecture or vendor changes
  - significant security incidents or control failures
  - major product/data processing changes
  - regulatory or contractual requirement changes

Annual baseline + trigger-based reassessments together form Kahana's risk management cadence.

5. Methodology (How Risks Are Assessed)
Kahana follows a structured methodology:

5.1 Scope and Context Definition
- Define assessment scope (systems, data, processes, vendors)
- Identify business/service commitments and critical assets

5.2 Asset and Process Identification
- List key assets and processes (for example: auth, data storage, CI/CD, logging, backup/restore, incident response, vendor dependencies)

5.3 Threat and Vulnerability Identification
- Identify realistic threats (unauthorized access, data exposure, misconfiguration, outages, dependency/vendor failures, insider misuse, phishing, etc.)
- Identify known control gaps and vulnerabilities

5.4 Risk Analysis and Scoring
For each risk, score:
- Likelihood: Low / Medium / High
- Impact: Low / Medium / High
- Inherent risk rating derived from likelihood + impact

Then document:
- Existing controls
- Residual risk (expected after mitigation)

5.4.1 Scoring Definitions (Kahana Standard)
Likelihood:
- Low: Unlikely given current controls; no recent related incidents; requires multiple control failures
- Medium: Credible scenario under current operating conditions; may occur occasionally
- High: Expected or likely without immediate control improvement; known gaps or repeated indicators exist

Impact:
- Low: Limited operational impact; minimal customer effect; no material compliance concern
- Medium: Noticeable service/customer impact; internal escalation required; potential contractual/compliance implications
- High: Significant confidentiality/integrity/availability impact; material customer impact and/or regulatory/contractual exposure

5.4.2 Inherent Risk Matrix
Use this matrix before considering new mitigation actions:

| Likelihood \\ Impact | Low | Medium | High |
|----------------------|-----|--------|------|
| Low                  | Low | Low-Medium | Medium |
| Medium               | Low-Medium | Medium | High |
| High                 | Medium | High | High |

Normalization rule:
- Low-Medium values are recorded as Medium for prioritization unless leadership documents an explicit rationale to keep them as Low.

5.4.3 Residual Risk
Residual risk is reassessed after planned controls are implemented:
- If treatment is complete and effective evidence exists, reduce rating accordingly.
- If treatment is partially complete or evidence is missing, do not reduce residual risk below Medium without Security Lead approval.

5.5 Risk Treatment Decision
Each risk must be assigned one or more treatment decisions:
- Mitigate
- Accept
- Transfer
- Avoid

Treatment decisions must include owners, target dates, and evidence references.

5.6 Leadership Review and Approval
Assessment outcomes are reviewed with Security Lead and leadership (CTO/CEO), with approval of:
- prioritized risks
- accepted risks
- treatment plans and timelines

6. Risk Rating Guidance
Use consistent rating criteria:
- High risk: likely and/or high business/customer impact; requires prompt mitigation and leadership visibility
- Medium risk: meaningful impact with manageable exposure; mitigation planned/tracked
- Low risk: limited impact/likelihood; may be accepted with periodic review

High risks should not be accepted without explicit leadership approval and documented rationale.

7. Required Outputs and Artifacts
Each formal assessment produces or updates:
- Risk Assessment Report
- Information Security Risk Register
- Risk Treatment Plan

These documents must be internally consistent (IDs, ratings, owners, actions, statuses).

8. Roles and Responsibilities
- Security Lead / Compliance Lead:
  - owns assessment process, scoring consistency, and report quality
- Engineering / DevOps / System Owners:
  - provide technical input, control status, and remediation plans
- Vendor Owner / Legal (as needed):
  - provide vendor risk and contractual dependency input
- CTO / CEO:
  - review and approve risk posture and treatment priorities

9. Tracking and Follow-Up
Risk treatment actions must be tracked to completion in the Risk Register/Treatment Plan with:
- status updates (Open, In Progress, Mitigated, Accepted)
- target and completion dates
- evidence links where applicable

Progress should be reviewed at least quarterly for high/medium risks.

10. Integration with Other Policies
Risk assessment outputs must inform and be informed by:
- Access Control Policy
- Logging & Monitoring Policy
- Backup and Restoration Policy
- Incident Response Plan
- Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery plans
- Vendor Management Policy

11. Evidence and Audit Readiness
For SOC 2 readiness, Kahana retains evidence of:
- annual assessment execution
- leadership review/approval
- risk register updates
- treatment progress and completed remediation

Evidence should be stored in approved internal documentation/evidence locations.

12. Exceptions
Any exception to this policy (for example, delayed assessment or deferred treatment) must be:
- documented with justification and risk impact
- approved by Security Lead and CTO
- time-bound with an explicit follow-up date

13. Review and Maintenance
This policy is reviewed at least annually and updated when risk methodology, architecture, or compliance obligations materially change.
