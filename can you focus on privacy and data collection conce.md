# can you focus on privacy and data collection concerns around google chrome? provide statistics and source urls

Chrome is one of the most data‑hungry mainstream browsers, and multiple recent sources tie its design directly to extensive data collection and ad/AI profiling rather than just “showing you the web.”[^1][^2][^3]

## How much data Chrome collects

- Recent research finds that Chrome is “the most data‑hungry browser,” collecting 20 different types of user data across numerous categories, while the average competing browser collects only about 6 data types.[^2]
Full URL: https://surfshark.com/research/chart/data-collection-mobile-browsers[^2]
- A 2025 analysis notes that Chrome’s mobile browser plus its integrated Gemini AI agent collect 24 different data types directly linked to the user, including name, precise location, device ID, browsing and search history, product interactions, and purchase history.[^4]
Full URL: https://surfshark.com/research/chart/gemini-chrome-user-data-collection[^4]
- Chrome is singled out as the only major mobile browser that both collects financial information (payment methods, card numbers, bank account details) and a list of contacts from the user’s phone or address book.[^2]
Full URL: https://surfshark.com/research/chart/data-collection-mobile-browsers[^2]


## Types of personal data pulled into Chrome

A 2025 deep‑dive on Chrome’s data collection and advertising practices (desktop + mobile) summarizes 8 major categories of data Chrome can ingest:[^1]

- Identifiers: user ID, device ID, unique browser identifiers.[^1]
- Contact info: your own contact details plus, uniquely, your phone’s contact list.[^1]
- Financial info: payment methods, card numbers, and bank account details.[^1]
- Location: both coarse and precise location information.[^1]
- Browsing activity: full browsing history, search terms, and sites visited.[^1]
- User content: audio data, customer support interactions, content you create or share.[^1]
- Usage data: feature usage, product interactions, how you use Chrome.[^1]
- Diagnostics: crash reports, performance metrics, technical logs.[^1]

Full URL: https://kahana.io/blog/google-chrome-data-collection-advertising-practices-2025[^1]

That write‑up also notes that Chrome stores your browsing history for 90 days by default and links much of this data directly to you when you’re signed in, rather than keeping it anonymous.[^1]

## Linking Chrome data to Google’s ad/AI business

- A 2026 review of big‑tech data practices finds that Google collects “the most data” of any major tech company, including location, browsing history, interactions after you click a Google link, and all data stored across Google services, and that Chrome‑synced browsing history feeds directly into your Google profile.[^3]
Full URL: https://www.security.org/resources/data-tech-companies-have/[^3]
- A 2025 Chrome analysis ties this browser‑level collection to Google’s roughly 230–240 billion‑dollar annual advertising business, arguing that Chrome’s default telemetry and account sync are designed to maximize how much behavioral data can be used for targeting and model training.[^1]
Full URL: https://kahana.io/blog/google-chrome-data-collection-advertising-practices-2025[^1]
- Earlier reporting comparing Chrome to Safari, Edge, and Firefox notes that, unlike those browsers, Chrome explicitly links nearly all harvested categories (browsing history, usage stats, location) to individuals and devices, rather than keeping at least some data unlinked.[^5]
Full URL: https://www.forbes.com/sites/zakdoffman/2021/03/20/stop-using-google-chrome-on-apple-iphone-12-pro-max-ipad-and-macbook-pro/[^5]


## Tracking, cookies, and “secret” history leaks

- In April 2025, Google confirmed that a long‑standing browser behavior allowed websites to infer parts of your browsing history by checking which links appeared “visited,” a mechanism that trackers and attackers had abused for years; Chrome 136 will change this so sites can only see visited links within their own domain.[^6]
Full URL: https://www.forbes.com/sites/zakdoffman/2025/04/15/google-confirms-secret-chrome-tracking-how-to-stop-it/[^6]
- In April 2025, Google also announced it would *keep* third‑party cookies in Chrome rather than fully deprecating them, instead offering users more granular settings, meaning cross‑site tracking via cookies remains a default part of the Chrome ecosystem that users must actively manage.[^7][^8]
Full URLs:
    - https://privacysandbox.google.com/blog/privacy-sandbox-next-steps[^7]
    - https://www.didomi.io/blog/google-chrome-third-party-cookies-april-2025[^8]
