# Monthly Executive Summary: January 2026

## **Overview**
January 2026 marked a pivotal transition for Oasis Browser, evolving from a core product development phase into a high-momentum "Market-Ready" posture. We successfully scaled the team, solidified our mission, and validated significant enterprise demand through our first Sales Qualified Lead (SQL).

---

## **Key Progress & Milestones**

### **1. Product & Engineering**
*   **Windows Ecosystem:** Achieved critical momentum on the Windows production build, with over 60 dedicated engineering hours in the final two weeks alone. We are now on the verge of a cross-platform (Mac & Windows) PM release.
*   **Sprint Completion:** Successfully completed multiple sprints including Sprint 12 (AI Command for Native Splitview - Ashwin), Sprint 14 (Webpage Summarization - Rushyanth, Ashwin, Saideep, Pournami, Naveen), Sprint 16 (Gemini Model Migration - Rushyanth), and Sprint 20 (Split View Improvements). Rushyanth resolved Sprint 8 tab group issues including tab content querying, filtering, and compound commands.
*   **Sprint Organization:** Established clear team member assignments across 15 active sprints, enabling parallel development tracks and reducing merge conflicts. Team assignments include: Sprint 5 (Durgesh), Sprint 8 (Afshaan), Sprint 9 (Pournami, Saideep, Durgesh), Sprint 10 (Pournami, Saideep), Sprint 11 (Ashwin, Likhitha), Sprint 12 (Ashwin), Sprint 13 (Ashwin, Saideep, Pournami), Sprint 15 (Afshaan), Sprint 17 (Mohammad, Revanth, Kaushik, Atharva), Sprint 18 (Rushyanth).
*   **Feedback Integration:** Integrated 37 new feedback items from February 2026 into sprint structure, bringing total feedback items to 167. Created 7 new sprints (Sprints 18-24) to address new feedback themes including voice dictation improvements, feedback modal implementation, window management, and search/command interpretation.
*   **Design & UI:** Ashwin successfully synchronized the browser codebase with Pournami's high-fidelity Figma designs, bringing a new level of "elegance" to the interface that aligns with our core mission.

### **2. Sales & Marketing (The "Enterprise Pivot")**
*   **Lead Generation:** Qualified **27 Marketing Qualified Leads (MQLs)** in January and **1 Sales Qualified Lead (SQL)**. High-intent interest came from industry giants including **Meta, Walgreens, Glean, and Charles Schwab**. February 2026 week saw **8 MQLs** with continued enterprise interest.
*   **Culture Amp Proposal:** Engaged Julian Brennan (Culture Amp) with a strategic proposal for a managed browser pilot. This has defined a new high-value use case: providing secure SaaS access for short-term consultants without shipping hardware.
*   **Growth:** Total waitlist signups reached **145** (up from 141 in January), supported by a marketing engine generating strong impressions. Week of February 2 achieved **77.2K impressions** with **26.5% growth** in marketing reach.

### **3. Team & Operations**
*   **Scaling:** Onboarded 5 new members (**Ammemah, Ruturaj, Ravi, Ankit, and Yaseer**) across Product, Engineering, and Marketing.
*   **Participation:** Team engagement in time logging increased significantly, reaching **50-62.5%** in the final weeks of the month.

Sprint 17 (Chromium Enterprise Browser) is actively in development with a dedicated team (Mohammad, Revanth, Kaushik, Atharva) working on SSO integration, Chromium engine integration, and enterprise policy management.

### **3. Sprint Assignment Completion**
Several new sprints (19, 22, 23, 24) from February 2026 feedback still need team member assignments. These sprints address critical UX improvements including feedback modal implementation, search/command interpretation fixes, and window management improvements.
