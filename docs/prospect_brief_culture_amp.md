## Prospect Brief: Culture Amp

### 1. Company Snapshot

- **Company**: Culture Amp  
- **Category**: Employee experience / engagement / performance & people analytics  
- **Scale**:
  - ~1,000–1,100 employees (global SaaS, multi-team, multi-region complexity)
  - Estimated **$150–200M** annual revenue
  - **$400M+** total funding raised
  - **6,500–7,500+** customer companies and ~**25M** employees on the platform

**Implication**: Late‑stage, scaled SaaS business used to enterprise‑grade vendors, long evaluations, and rigorous security and ROI expectations.

---

### 2. Ideal Customer Profile & Customers

- **Industries**: Over‑indexed in:
  - Computer software (~19%)
  - IT services (~8%)
  - Internet (~7%)
  - Financial services (~5%)
- **Geography**:
  - ~60% customers in the US
  - ~12% Australia
  - ~8% UK
- **Company size**:
  - ~55% mid‑market
  - ~29% large enterprises (>1,000 employees)
  - Remaining smaller companies

**Takeaway**: Their ICP is mid‑market and enterprise, SaaS‑heavy, knowledge‑worker orgs—exactly the environment where an enterprise/AI‑native browser and secure productivity tooling makes sense.

---

### 3. Pricing Envelope & Economic Context

- **Model**: Per‑employee‑per‑month (PEPM) across Engage, Perform, Develop, People Analytics; custom enterprise bundles.
- **Indicative pricing**:
  - Typical PEPM range cited: **~$9–14**+
  - Annual contracts from **~$4.5k** up to **$40–45k+** depending on employee count and modules.

**Implication**: They are comfortable with **multi‑tens‑of‑thousands ACVs**, multi‑stakeholder evaluations, and value‑based pricing. They will expect clear ROI, not budget positioning alone.

---

### 4. Buying Centers & Stakeholders

- **Primary buyers**:
  - CHRO, VP People, Head of People Analytics, HR Directors
- **Key influencers**:
  - **CFO/COO**: ROI, efficiency, impact on performance
  - **IT/Security**: data protection, SSO/IdP, integrations, compliance
- **Daily users**:
  - HRBPs, HR Ops, People Analytics teams, managers, and employees

**Relevance for Oasis**: Messaging around **reducing context switching for HR and managers**, **making people data actionable in‑workflow**, and **secure, AI‑assisted browser workflows** will resonate.

---

### 5. Tech Stack Position & Sensitivities

- **Stack position**:
  - Sits alongside / on top of HRIS (Workday, BambooHR, HiBob, ADP, etc.)
  - Integrates with major HR systems via APIs for employee data and identity
- **Orientation**:
  - Strong on people analytics, benchmarks, ROI, and research‑backed insights
  - High sensitivity to **data quality, reporting, security, and compliance**

**Implication**: Any browser or AI tool must:
- Respect and enhance existing SSO/HRIS integrations  
- Provide strong security, privacy, and logging guarantees  
- Support analytics‑driven narratives (e.g., time saved, improved adoption, reduction in tool sprawl)

---

### 6. Wedge Hypotheses for Oasis / Enterprise Browser

- **Internal usage wedge**
  - Large, globally distributed internal teams (product, sales, CS, people science, marketing) who live in the browser all day.
  - Likely challenges:
    - Browser tab chaos, context switching across HRIS, analytics dashboards, internal tools, and docs
    - Security concerns around AI usage and sensitive people data in browsers
  - **Oasis angle**: AI‑native, secure browser that:
    - Reduces context switching for HR/People teams and managers
    - Provides safe, governed AI assistance on top of people data
    - Gives Security/IT better visibility and control over browser‑based workflows

- **Customer‑facing wedge**
  - Their customers are exactly the type of knowledge‑worker orgs that struggle with productivity and secure browser workflows.
  - **Potential partnership angle**: Explore co‑selling or integration concepts where:
    - Culture Amp insights surface in browser‑native workflows
    - Oasis provides secure, guided execution on top of people‑data insights.

---

### 7. Suggested First‑Contact Angles

- **Executive / Strategy angle (CHRO / VP People / People Analytics)**
  - “How are your own managers and HR teams using your insights day‑to‑day? We’re seeing that context switching across HRIS, analytics, and collaboration tools is a major drag on actually acting on people data.”

- **IT / Security angle**
  - “You’re a people‑data company with distributed, browser‑native teams. How are you thinking about securing AI usage and sensitive HR data in the browser, especially on remote and BYOD devices?”

- **Joint value angle**
  - “Culture Amp helps orgs know what should change; Oasis can help make those changes easier to execute securely in daily browser workflows for managers and HR teams.”
