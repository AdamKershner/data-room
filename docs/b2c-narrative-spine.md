## One-page arc (executive summary)

Creator-facing stage guide (generic Andy Raskin pedagogy): `docs/marketing/story-framework-stage-guide.md`: collapsed **Learn how to create content for this stage** on the checklist; Oasis voice stays in section intros and copy banks below.

It is 2026, and the default browser experience has become **Ad Nauseam**: an endless auction of ads and data sips that turned a work portal into a trap. You lose attention, deep work, and the memory of what focus felt like. The world already proved how far prediction goes. Target in 2012, before the browser became everyone’s dashboard.

The **Chrome Colossus** sets the environment: monoculture share, ad empire, antitrust pressure, and security cracks, the structural villain behind the drone.

A **fork** opens: **quiet rebels** treat the swarm as an attack surface; those who **stay on default** pay a cognition tax until they feel numb. Recognition lands: Ad Nauseam is the *feeling*; the structural truth is that **your browser works for Google**, not metaphorically, for ads, profiles, and training.

Then comes **imagination**: a codename **REFUGE**, a calmer browser that protects attention, keeps data yours, and helps without hijacking you. Pre-launch voice is “imagine”; no product name yet.

**Magical gifts are mapped to Oasis v1.0.0.12** ([product index](product/oasis-capability-index.md)) with one **gap/reframe** row (contacts in profile). Product facts: [b2c-magical-gifts-mapping.md](marketing/b2c-magical-gifts-mapping.md) · Publishable copy: [b2c-magic-gifts-copy.md](b2c-magic-gifts-copy.md).

**Conclusion:** You lived Ad Nauseam; the noise thins. **Relax, you found the Oasis**, stillness, room to breathe, an Oasis for you. Evidence and launch assets prove the story later; this spine is the emotional through-line.

## Name the change: Name a big, relevant change in the world

**Andy Raskin role:** Name the villain and the change in the world, why the old game is over.

### Canonical intro (checklist)

It is 2026, and we are in the thick of the Rise of Ad Nauseam. The browser used to be a portal to the vast universe of the internet, a tool for work, research, and the projects you cared about. That is the change we are naming: not that you are careless online, but that the default experience rewired itself while we were still mourning the old one.

The villain is not a single ad; it is Ad Nauseam itself. The tab you opened for work becomes a trap: every scroll, click, and pause logged and auctioned. This does not kick down the door; it seeps in: one more pre-roll, one more sidebar, one more “personalized” shoe. Big-tech mosquitoes sip your data while you scroll until the feed feels less like a window and more like a trap.

You cannot get a real breath for authentic deep work, the research paper, the company you are building, the writing project. Your mental RAM goes to ignoring instead of creating. You walk toward the browser like a bug zapper: booby-trapped corridors of billboards on every path to focus. You forgot what clarity felt like outside Ad Nauseam.

We are not inventing how far this goes. In 2012, [Target knew a teen was pregnant before her father did](https://www.forbes.com/sites/kashmirhill/2012/02/16/how-target-figured-out-a-teen-girl-was-pregnant-before-her-father-did/), prediction and profiling before the browser became everyone's daily dashboard. That is the world we are living in now.

The colossus behind the monoculture comes next, then the fork: who treats Ad Nauseam as backdrop, and who treats the swarm as an attack surface.

### Structured beats (outline: detail in copy bank)

1. **The Villain: Ad Nauseam**: trap, seep-in, highest bidder  
2. **The Mosquitoes**: bites, welts, data swarm under the scroll  
3. **The Cost**: field of view, mental RAM, priority erosion  
4. **The Fallen Portal**: portal → bug zapper, booby-trapped corridor  
5. **The Consequence**: forgot deep focus, half-open tabs  
6. **Handoff to Name the villain**: environment not willpower  

### Key vocabulary

Trap, seep-in, drone, mosquitoes, welts, mental RAM, bug zapper, booby-trapped corridor, portal → trap, forgot deep work, Rise of Ad Nauseam

- No Product Hunt Brief 01, no “works for Google” hook, no Oasis pitch  
- No full winners/losers thesis (that is Winners and losers)  
- No Chrome monopoly stats (that is Name the villain)

## Name the villain: Name the villain (Chrome Colossus)

**Andy Raskin role:** Name the **structural** villain, the environment Ad Nauseam breathes in: Chrome as monoculture, ad stack gateway, antitrust target, and breach surface.

### Canonical intro (checklist)

Chrome’s rise is a villain origin story: fast simple browser → default monoculture (~68% global share; Firefox ~2%). Chrome is the front door to Google’s ad stack, majority ad revenue, incentive to mine every scroll. The state names it too (DOJ 2025 ad-tech win; June 2025 Ads breach ~2.5M records). The colossus is the **environment**, next: who acts before the default finishes selling them out.

### Key vocabulary

Chrome monoculture, ad machine, skin of the ad machine, identity graph, antitrust, concentrated target

### What we do not say in Name the villain

- No Oasis product pitch  
- No “we defeated Chrome”  
- No unverified breakup claims

## Winners and losers: Show there'll be winners and losers

**Andy Raskin role:** Show the fork, two futures, stakes as futures not blame.

### Canonical intro (checklist)

Winners stop treating Ad Nauseam as background radiation and start treating the swarm as an attack surface. They are the quiet rebels: they notice when the internet feels like a rigged casino, they budget attention like money, they reduce their tracking surface, and they choose tools that do not live on surveillance. Mosquitoes only win when you keep sitting outside without a net.

Losers are not stupid; they stay on default. They tell themselves that is just how the internet works, pay a cognition tax on every task, and leave every switch on. Big-tech mosquitoes sip while they scroll until they are a hollow husk; time stretches thin, then ad after ad; identity splinters until nothing sacred is left to hold for themselves.

Over time, losers feel numbness instead of outrage: the swarm feels inevitable. Winners regain headspace and agency; losers become background to someone else's optimization story. The fork is not blame; it is whether you act before the default browser finishes selling you out.

### Bridge beat (recognition: narrative only)

After the villain and fork, a **bridge** post assumes readers felt the drone and mosquitoes. Pivot:

- *Ad Nauseam is the feeling.*  
- *Here is the structural truth: your browser works for Google, for ads, profiles, and AI training. You've noticed it. The ad that followed the search. The feed that knew too much.*  
- Close with recognition, not a product CTA yet: *When did you first notice your browser wasn't on your side?*

Maps to checklist task `mn-ph-chrome-01` (lives in **Winners and losers**, not Name the change).

### Key vocabulary

Quiet rebels, attack surface, rigged casino, cognition tax, hollow husk, identity splinters, numbness vs agency, optimization story

Split-screen “Working for Google / Working for you” and PH captions are **launch assets** (`mn-ph-chrome-02`, `mn-ph-chrome-03`), binary contrast on May 27, 2026, not the emotional essay in this doc.

## Promised Land: Tease the Promised Land (REFUGE)

**Andy Raskin role:** Paint the Promised Land readers can imagine, relief after Ad Nauseam and the fork.

### Canonical intro (checklist)

Imagine a refuge built for people exhausted by noisy tabs, distracting feeds, and software that constantly demands attention. A calmer way to move online, private by default, elegant by design, powered by intelligence that helps without getting in the way. The more you live inside it, the more it understands what matters to you.

Most of what you use today competes for your attention. A refuge should protect it. Privacy first. Clean, focused, intelligently adaptive. You move faster without being overwhelmed, while your data remains fully yours, always.

We believe the internet should feel like clarity, not chaos. A refuge that combines privacy, elegant design, and personal intelligence, something that adapts to you over time, remembers what matters, removes friction, and helps you stay in flow without compromising ownership of your data.

Imagine a refuge where your browsing history helps you on your machine, not a profile auctioned to advertisers. Imagine help in the sidebar that improves the product without tying every prompt to your identity unless you choose that.

### Product-backed promises (v1.0.0.12)

From [oasis-your-data-and-training.md](marketing/oasis-your-data-and-training.md): **REFUGE voice in tease; Oasis name only at launch.**

1. **Browsing stays on your machine**: history, bookmarks, and searchable memory on device; not sold as an ad profile (`mn-promised-chrome-06`).
2. **Assistant on your terms**: improvement logging anonymous by default; personalize opt-in only (`mn-promised-chrome-07`).
3. **Honest boundaries**: anonymous ≠ zero data; ETP = fewer tracker bites, not zero ads; does not block Gemini-in-Chrome.

**Handoff to Magic gifts:** Gift 2 (`mn-gifts-chrome-02`) proves the assistant promise with named Oasis product truth.

### REFUGE tease variants (pre-launch: codename only)

**Variant 1: Calmer browse:** Imagine a browser built for people exhausted by noisy tabs, distracting feeds, and software that constantly demands attention. It's a calmer way to browse, private by default, elegant by design, and powered by AI that helps without getting in the way. The more you use it, the more it understands what matters to **you**.

**Variant 2: Protects attention:** Most browsers compete for your attention. Imagine one that **protects** it. Designed around privacy first, REFUGE cuts through the noise with an experience that feels clean, focused, and intelligently adaptive. AI helps you move faster without overwhelming you, while your data remains fully yours, always.

**Variant 3: Clarity, not chaos:** We believe browsers should feel like places of clarity, not chaos. Imagine combining privacy, elegant design, and personal AI into a browsing experience that adapts to **you** over time. It remembers what matters, removes friction, and helps you stay in flow, without compromising ownership of your data.

**Imagine frame:** *Imagine a browser built like a refuge, not a billboard.*

### Launch only: not tease (narrative turn to Oasis)

On Product Hunt (May 27, 2026), the codename becomes the product name. Example launch voice (description B):

Most browsers compete for your attention. **Oasis** protects it. Designed around privacy first, Oasis cuts through the noise with an experience that feels clean, focused, and intelligently adaptive. AI helps you move faster without overwhelming you, while your data remains fully yours, always.

Binary hook on launch assets only: *Your browser works for Google. Ours works for you.* See [product-hunt-brief-01-privacy-angle.md](product-hunt-brief-01-privacy-angle.md).

## Magic gifts: Magical gifts: narrative role + feature gap

**Andy Raskin role:** Introduce “magic gifts”, each removes **one obstacle** on the path to the Promised Land. Observable contrast, not a feature dump.

**Narrative position:** After REFUGE imagination, before Present evidence proof. The reader should feel: *something concrete could remove this pain.* Capabilities below are tied to **v1.0.0.12** codebase docs, honest about **shipped**, **partial**, and **gap**.

### Checklist · Magic gifts intro (canonical)

You imagined a refuge, a browser that protects attention instead of auctioning it. Ad Nauseam does not vanish because you pictured something better. The air is still thick with the drone, the mosquitoes, the tab that became a trap. **Magic gifts** are the observable steps off that path: each one removes **one obstacle** on the way to the Promised Land, not willpower lectures, not a feature dump. One pain, one gift, one old world → new world.

This refuge is not built on an ads business. We are not here to auction your attention to the highest bidder like the Chrome colossus behind the ad machine. It is **free forever**: the product wins when it makes you more productive, faster work, fewer interruptions, better outcomes, not when it sells more impressions.

You browse with **Enhanced Tracking Protection** on, built on Firefox, always on guard. Cross-site trackers and follow-you stitching thin out; ads may still appear, and we say so. You stop fighting the swarm in your head and start noticing your own ideas again.

You use **clarification** to confirm what you meant before the browser acts. You say “Group my research tabs” and the assistant organizes windows for you. You use **close duplicate tabs**, **summarize page**, and **search memory** to finish the work you opened the browser for, not twenty tabs later. You run the browser in natural language.

You thumbs-up a reply to **train** the assistant and choose **anonymous** training so feedback sharpens workflows without tying every correction to your account. You leave **Personalize the assistant with my account** off by default: help in the sidebar without opting into a linked pipeline on day one. Over time you fall in love with an assistant that understands how you speak, learns from your choices when you allow it, and becomes the reason this browser feels like yours: familiar, capable, and yours alone, without giving away what makes you, you.

- Lead with **You use [feature] to [outcome]**; one obstacle → one gift per post.
